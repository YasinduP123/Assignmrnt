Asteroid

Asteroids are rocky objects orbiting the Sun, primarily found in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter. Varying in size from tiny pebbles to hundreds of kilometers in diameter, they are remnants from the early solar system, offering clues about its formation. Unlike comets, asteroids lack significant ice, resulting in no visible coma or tail. Some asteroids have moons or form binary systems. Studying asteroids helps scientists understand planetary formation and the potential for resource utilization in future space missions.


Satellite

A satellite is an artificial object placed in orbit around Earth or another celestial body to collect information or for communication purposes. Satellites are used in various fields, including meteorology, navigation, television broadcasting, and scientific research. They provide crucial data for weather forecasting, GPS services, and environmental monitoring. Satellites can be classified into categories such as geostationary, low Earth orbit (LEO), and medium Earth orbit (MEO), depending on their altitude and function. Advances in satellite technology continue to revolutionize how we observe and interact with our planet, enabling global connectivity and enhancing our understanding of the universe.

Solar System

Our solar system comprises the Sun and the celestial bodies that orbit it, including eight planets: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. It also contains dwarf planets like Pluto, moons, asteroids, and comets. The Sun, a medium-sized star, provides the gravitational force that keeps the system together. The planets follow elliptical orbits, with the inner planets being rocky and the outer ones primarily gaseous. The asteroid belt lies between Mars and Jupiter, while the Kuiper Belt and Oort Cloud, home to many icy bodies, lie beyond Neptune. Our solar system formed about 4.6 billion years ago.

Galaxy

Galaxies are vast collections of stars, planets, gas, dust, and dark matter bound together by gravity. They come in various shapes and sizes, from spiral, elliptical, to irregular. Our Milky Way galaxy, home to our solar system, contains billions of stars, with the Sun being just one. Galaxies harbor diverse stellar populations and host phenomena like supernovae , black holes, and nebulae. They play a crucial role in the cosmic web, influencing the evolution of the universe. Studying galaxies offers insights into cosmology, galactic dynamics, and the nature of dark matter and dark energy, shaping our understanding of the cosmos.
